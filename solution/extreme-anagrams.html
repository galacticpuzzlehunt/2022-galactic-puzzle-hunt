Solution Extreme Anagrams
Answer HALLEY'S COMET / CHRYSLER

Written by Yannick Yao

This puzzle consists of several lines of anagrammed text, where the letters are sorted in alphabetical order but all other aspects (capitalizations, punctuations, spaces) are preserved. Some of the features are quite recognizable as coming from a well-known sentence, such as “N-O-O-O-R” almost certainly corresponding to “E-I-E-I-O” in the nursery rhyme Old Macdonald Had A Farm, and some lines containing every letter at least once must necessarily come from a pangram. Using the letter distributions and other features, we can gradually find out the original sentences, which are always some famous sentences, including lyrics/poems, sayings, tongue twisters, and quotes:

In addition, each line has a single underlined letter. Extracting the letter in the original sentence in the same position as the underlined letter gives ANSWER HALLEY’S COMET.

Now, notice that these lines are suspiciously given in alphabetical order by the original sentences, even though the extraction never required such ordering. Moreover, there are some punctuations (apostrophe at the end of line 4, parentheses at the start of line 14 and end of line 18) that do not appear in the original sentences, as well as two blank lines.

Given the theme of this puzzle, we can intuit that we now need to anagram the first letters of the original sentences, as if it was given in the form of CDEE’E EGHIJOPP (RRSTU), into a clue-phrase. Assuming that the word in parentheses is the enumeration of the answer, this anagrams into the clue-phrase JEEP’S PRODUCER (EIGHT). The current producer of Jeep SUVs is Stellantis, also known as CHRYSLER, so this is the second answer.

Author’s Notes

In puzzle hunts, unclued anagrams are almost always frowned upon, and even when clued they tend to be on words or short phrases. Therefore, I found it interesting to try making a puzzle explicitly about anagramming, especially with long strings that online tools (such as Nutrimatic) cannot solve. I tried to pick sentences with unusual features to make identification a bit easier, although some of them might still be difficult without tools.

Here are some sentences that didn’t make it into the puzzle, if you want some additional challenges:
