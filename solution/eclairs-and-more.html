Solution Eclairs and More @ Ovsljs* Opsvut
Answer GOODBYE, FAREWELL, AND AMEN / LYRIC

Written by Mark

We start by observing that the names of the menu items often end with the same five-letter word “OPSVU”, suggesting a substitution cipher. As suggested by the overall theming and title of the puzzle, the menu items are in fact enciphered names of donuts from Dunkin’ Donuts. However, as clued by the flavortext “the new flavors each kept something from the old ones”, each donut name has a letter that was copied directly from the original name, rather than substituted.

Upon deciphering the donut names, we observe that the menu items are ordered alphabetically by the donut names. At the same time, we identify the objects in each image and observe that the price of each menu item gives an enumeration for the object name. We notice that each object name starts with a different letter from A to V. Using this as a sorting order for the letters “kept the same” in the donut names, we arrive at the first answer GOODBYE, FAREWELL AND AMEN.

In looking for the second answer, we make one of several observations:

  • The images have so far been underconstrained. Studying the images more carefully, we notice that many of them are “holey”. In fact, they all have a well-defined number of holes, from a topological perspective, which is a rather unusual property.
  • The substitution cipher takes “B” to “&”, which look similar to each other because they both have two holes.
  • The rest of the substitution cipher contains two cycles in alphabetical order, one containing the letters “ADOPQR” and the other containing the rest of the alphabet. (It also takes apostrophes to asterisks and È to Ḟ.) Studying the letters “ADOPQR” carefully or entering “ADOPQR” into a search engine reveals that they form the set of letters with exactly one hole.
  • Donuts are canonically known for their holes.
  • A mug appears in the puzzle. A common joke states that donuts and mugs are the same, at least to topologists, because they both have one hole.
  • The flavortext mentions a “whole” revamp, which is a pun on “hole”.

Counting the number of holes in each object, indexing them into the donut names and sorting by object name gives the letters ITSTHEWORDSOFASONGFIVÈ, which we parse as the cluephrase IT’S THE WORDS OF A SONG (5). This clues the second answer, LYRIC.
